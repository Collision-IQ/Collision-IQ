## 2. Cold Email Objection-Handling Scripts

### Objection 1: *"We already use CCC ONE."*

**Strategic frame:** Never argue against CCC ONE — it's the industry's system of record and often contractually required by DRP agreements. Reposition: Collision IQ isn't competing for that seat; it eliminates the manual work that happens *before* CCC ONE gets involved.

**Email reply:**

> Subject: Re: Collision IQ — good, keep it
>
> {{first_name}} —
>
> Good — keep it. Collision IQ isn't a CCC replacement; it's the layer in front of it.
>
> Right now, someone at your shop is turning drop-off photos into CCC lines by hand: 30+ minutes per sheet, and the ADAS calibration behind that bumper cover doesn't show up until supplement two after teardown.
>
> Collision IQ reads the photos at intake, drafts the lines — parts, labor, refinish, calibration triggers — and hands your estimator structured output to drop into the platform you already run. Nothing about your DRP agreements or carrier uploads changes.
>
> Your writers stop transcribing and start estimating. Worth 15 minutes to see it run on your own photos?

**Live-call talk track (if it comes up on the phone):**
- Agree immediately: "You should be on CCC — most of our shops are."
- Pivot to the gap: "CCC is where the estimate *lives*. The question is how long it takes to get the estimate *into* it, and how much gets missed on the first write."
- Proof anchor: first-sheet ADAS/structural capture → fewer supplements → faster approvals → earlier parts orders → cycle time.

### Objection 2: *"Our insurance partners force us to use Mitchell."*

**Strategic frame:** Validate the constraint — DRP platform mandates are real and non-negotiable for the shop. Then separate the *platform requirement* (which Collision IQ doesn't touch) from the *estimating labor* (which it does).

**Email reply:**

> Subject: Re: Mitchell stays — that's the point
>
> {{first_name}} —
>
> Understood, and nothing changes there. Your carrier agreements dictate the platform of record; Collision IQ doesn't ask you to break them.
>
> What the carrier *doesn't* dictate is how the first draft gets built. Today that's an estimator keying lines manually — and the items Mitchell can't see from a desk (occupant-safety inspections, one-time-use fasteners, calibrations triggered by a windshield or bumper job) surface late, as supplements your DRP scorecard counts against you.
>
> Collision IQ drafts from intake photos and flags those items on sheet one. Your team reviews, adjusts, and submits through Mitchell exactly as they do now — just faster, with fewer supplements dragging your metrics.

> Happy to show it on a repair you've already closed, so you can compare against what the file actually caught. 15 minutes?

**Live-call talk track:**
- Never suggest going around the carrier. "Your DRP relationship is the asset — we make its scorecard better."
- Reframe supplements: each late supplement is approval lag + a scorecard hit + a parts order that started days late.
- If pressed on compatibility: output is structured estimate data your team enters/imports per their normal Mitchell workflow; the carrier sees the same platform, same process, better first submission.

## Why Collision Repairers Are Enhancing Their Estimating with Collision IQ

Collision repair shops aren't abandoning their estimating platforms — they're upgrading what happens before the first line is ever written. Collision IQ is AI collision estimating software that converts drop-off damage photos into draft estimate lines in seconds, then hands structured output to the platform of record your shop and your carriers already use.

### The first write is where estimates go wrong

Most estimate problems aren't typing errors — they're visibility problems. A photo estimate written from a desk can't see the one-time-use fasteners behind a bumper cover, the occupant-safety inspection an OE position statement requires, or the ADAS calibration triggered by a windshield replacement. Those items surface during teardown, become supplement two or three, and every late supplement means another approval cycle, a parts order that starts days late, and a DRP scorecard that drifts the wrong direction.

Collision IQ's computer vision analyzes intake photos panel by panel, classifies damage by type and severity, and cross-checks findings against repair logic and calibration triggers — so structural, safety, and ADAS operations land on the first sheet, not the third supplement. Anything the model can't verify from evidence is flagged for the estimator with a confidence score. It drafts; it doesn't guess.

### Built to enhance, not replace

Legacy estimating systems earned their place as the industry's platforms of record, and carrier agreements often require them. Collision IQ respects that reality. It operates as an overlay: photos in, structured estimate data out, delivered into your existing workflow. There's no data migration, no DRP re-enrollment, and no retraining your team on a new system of record. Shops are typically writing AI-drafted estimates within days of onboarding — with shop-specific and carrier-specific rule sets (included operations, documentation requirements, labor allowances) configured from day one.

### What shops measure after switching on Collision IQ

- **Estimator throughput.** Writers stop transcribing and start reviewing. The ~30 minutes of manual entry per initial estimate compresses to a review-and-adjust pass measured in minutes.
- **Supplement frequency.** ADAS calibrations, structural operations, and required inspections captured at intake mean fewer late supplements and faster carrier approvals.
- **Cycle time.** Earlier, more complete first estimates mean parts get ordered sooner, blueprinting starts sooner, and days-in-shop comes down — the metric every carrier scorecard, CSI survey, and cash-flow statement watches.
- **Consistency across writers.** The AI drafts the same complete first sheet whether it's your 30-year veteran or your second-year estimator holding the tablet.

Flat monthly pricing per location. No per-estimate metering, no multi-year lock-in, cancel anytime. You can calculate your ROI before you ever talk to us — and when you do, we'll run the demo on your own damage photos, not a canned example car.
