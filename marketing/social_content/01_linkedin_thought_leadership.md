## Post Body

The collision industry has a math problem nobody wants to say out loud:

Senior estimators are retiring faster than we're training replacements. The average estimator writing sheets today carries 15–20 years of teardown knowledge in their head — and when they leave, that knowledge leaves with them.

Here's what most shops get wrong about AI estimating: they think it's a replacement play.

It isn't. It's a leverage play.

A senior estimator using computer-vision triage doesn't write fewer estimates. They write **more accurate ones, faster**, because the software handles the part they hate — line-by-line data entry, looking up included operations, cross-checking p-pages — and leaves them the part that actually requires judgment:

→ Is that quarter panel a repair or a replace?
→ Does this OEM require a post-repair calibration the photo can't show?
→ What did the photos miss that a teardown will find?

The shops winning right now aren't replacing their best estimator. They're cloning their best estimator's output across every file — and letting the 30-year vet mentor the 2-year writer instead of both drowning in keystrokes.

AI doesn't close the talent gap. It changes how much talent one person can cover.
